About Professor Bass

William Bass is a forensic anthropologist who created the Anthropology Research Facility at the University of Tennessee. Bass served for two years in the US Army. After completing his masterâ€™s degree, Bass attended the University of Pennsylvania. He taught in the Anthropology Department at the University of Kansas. Later, Bass left the University of Kansas to serve as a professor of Anthropology at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. He established the Forensic Anthropology Center in 1987 and served as its director. He has served as professor emeritus at the University of Tennessee and presently teaches at Stoddard University.
